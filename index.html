My background

Black hole astrophysics, drug discovery, and machine learning

I develop and expand sophisticated tools for the analysis of complex data. I spent ten years working as a research astrophysicist at the University of Cambridge and the European Space Agency, using machine learning to understand accretion onto black holes.

Now, I build AI models to help chemists discover new drugs, by generating improved chemical compounds, predicting their properties, and optimising existing compounds.
