Michael Parker - AI Researcher, Data Scientist

Experience

Principal AI Scientist, Optibrium Ltd.

AI research for drug discovery, 2022 - present

  • Redesigned and implemented Bayesian inference engine for scoring compounds based on user feedback, reducing the codebase by 90% while improving the speed, flexibility and scaling to higher dimensions.
  • Designed and developed a guided transformer model (tensorflow) for generating new compounds with improved chemical properties.

    • Postdoctoral Research Associate, Institute of Astronomy, University of Cambridge

    Independent research, developing machine learning methods for astronomy, 2020 - 2022

  • Developed a deep learning model for dark matter detection and measurement in complex X-ray spectra, giving a 3x improvement in sensitivity at low computational cost.
  • Designed and implemented a spectral analysis pipeline using combined PCA (scipy and sklearn) and multilayer perceptron (keras/tensorflow), offering a 1000Ã— improvement in speed and comparable parameter estimation accuracy to conventional spectroscopy.
  • Supervised two masters students and two summer projects on various topics, including applying Gaussian processes and exoplanet detection tools (PyMC3) to black hole time series, implementing a new Gaussian sampling technique to remove noise from Monte-Carlo models, exploring very high frequency Fourier analysis of black hole data.
  • Led a major systematic study of degeneracies between leading spectral models, based on extensive simulations, and including various mitigation strategies.

    • European Space Agency Research Fellow, ESAC, Madrid

    Independent research fellowship, time series modelling, 2017-2020

  • Developed a new modelling scheme for time series data (python/astropy), allowing new parameters to be constrained and new physics to be tested. Expanded on this in multiple projects, applying the models to various different physical regimes, and created a public library of the resulting models for community use.
  • Led multiple international teams to be awarded 180 hours of telescope time on orbiting observatories (approximate financial value of $600000), to study various topics in black hole accretion.
  • Discovered and pioneered a new method for detection of black hole outflows from X-ray data, using enhanced variability signatures in time series.
  • Supervised two ESA trainees on data analysis and modelling projects I designed, both of whom published papers on their work and were accepted to prestigious PhD programs

    • Postdoctoral Research Associate, Institute of Astronomy, University of Cambridge

    Independent research, spectroscopic and time series data modelling, 2016-2017

  • Led major international data analysis team to discover variable behaviour of outflows from supermassive black holes, published in Nature.
